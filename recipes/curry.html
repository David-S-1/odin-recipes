Curry

Description

Chicken curry or curry/curried chicken is a South Asian dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is common in the cuisine of the Indian subcontinent, Caribbean, Southeast Asia, Great Britain, and Kenya.

Ingredients

Chicken
  • 21 oz. Chicken Thighs
  • 2 Tbsp Cooking Oil
  • 2 Tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 Tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 Tsp Tumeric/Curry Powder
  • 1 Tsp Salt
  • 1/2 Tsp Red Chili Powder

Sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Cooking Oil
  • 2 Tbsp Butter
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp Minced Garlic
  • 1 Tbsp Minced Ginger
  • 1 1/2 Tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 1/2 Tsp Cumin
  • 1 Tsp Ground Corriander
  • 1 Tsp Tumeric Powder/Curry Powder
  • 15 oz. can of Crushed Tomatoes
  • 1 Tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 Tsp Salt
  • 1 Tsp Brown Sugar
  • 13.5 fl oz. can of Coconut Milk/Coconut Cream
  • Finely chopped Cilantro

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, toss chicken with 2 tablespoons of the oil and spices to evenly coat.
  2. Heat remaining oil in a large pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken in batches until browned, about 3-4 minutes each side. Set aside and keep warm. (The chicken will finish cooking in the sauce.)
  3. Melt the butter in the same pan. Fry onion until soft (about 3 minutes), scraping up any browned bits on the bottom of the pan.
  4. Add garlic and ginger; saut√© until fragrant, about for 1 minute. Add in garam masala, cumin, ground coriander and turmeric (or curry powder). Cook stirring occasionally until fragrant, about 20 seconds.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes, chili powder, salt and sugar. Let simmer until slightly thickened, about 4 minutes.
  6. Stir in coconut milk/cream. Add chicken and juices back into the pan. Let simmer until chicken is cooked through and the sauce is thick, about 5 minutes.
  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with fresh garlic butter rice and homemade naan bread.
